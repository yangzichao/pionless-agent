# Framing Checklist

Before searching, write down four things. Two sentences each, max.

1. **The exact question.** One sentence, no compound clauses. If you need "and" to express it, you have two questions — pick one or escalate to deep-research.
2. **The shape of a good-enough answer.** A number? A yes/no? A 3-item comparison? A short paragraph?
3. **Constraints.** Recency window, geography, domain, jurisdiction, version, vendor.
4. **Out of scope.** What you are explicitly *not* answering, so the search does not drift.

If you cannot fill (1) and (2) cleanly, the question is too broad for this skill. Two valid responses:

- Ask the user to narrow it.
- Tell the host this is a deep-research case, not a quick-research case, and stop.

Do not silently expand scope to cover an ambiguous prompt. Quick research is fast because it is narrow.
