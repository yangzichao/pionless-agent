# Writing Guidelines

## Answer-first structure

Lead every section with the conclusion, then support it. The reader should be able to act on the Executive Summary alone.

## Citation density

Pro-tier reports are citation-dense. Every load-bearing claim should carry an inline citation. If a paragraph contains five claims, expect five citations.

## Source vs inference

Distinguish sourced facts from inference. Use phrases like:

- "based on [source]" for sourced facts,
- "this suggests" or "this implies" for inference,
- "[author] argues that" for cited opinion.

Never silently elevate an inference to a stated fact.

## Mandatory pro-tier sections

Pro-tier reports must include:

- **Methodology** — search approach, sources consulted, and any methodological limitations.
- **Contradictions & Contested Claims** — where sources disagree, the disagreement, and which side the evidence favors.
- **Limitations** — what was not resolved and why.

## Decision-readiness

The report exists to support a decision. Every section should serve that goal.

- Findings → directly answer subquestions.
- Comparison tables → side-by-side decision support.
- Contradictions → flag where the answer is genuinely uncertain.
- Open Risks → flag what could change the answer.
- Limitations → what the report cannot conclude.

## Style

- Compact. Avoid walls of prose; use tables and lists where they fit.
- Inline citations as `[Source Title](url)`.
- One claim per bullet in evidence lists.
- No filler ("It's worth noting that..."); state it directly.
