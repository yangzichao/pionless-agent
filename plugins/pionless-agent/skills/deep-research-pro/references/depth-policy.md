# Depth Policy

Use iterative deepening **without artificial depth caps**. Each subquestion has a depth target on the plan board; depth ceiling is determined by evidence saturation, not a fixed number.

## Depth ladder

- **Depth 0.** Frame the problem and outline the answer shape.
- **Depth 1.** Establish main claims with sources.
- **Depth 2.** Verify contested or decision-critical claims with 3+ independent sources.
- **Depth 3.** Cross-reference across domains, resolve contradictions, examine minority viewpoints.
- **Depth 4+.** Pursue remaining uncertainties, edge cases, and methodological critiques.

## Pro-tier guidance

Most major subquestions should reach depth 3 at this tier. Depth 4+ is appropriate when:

- the decision stakes justify it,
- contradictions remain unresolved,
- minority viewpoints have not yet been examined,
- methodological critiques of the primary sources have not yet been considered.

## Stopping per subquestion

Stop deepening on a subquestion when:

- the evidence is saturated (3 consecutive queries return no new facts),
- the gate criteria for that subquestion are met with 3+ sources,
- the subquestion is downgraded on the plan board.

Do not stop on iteration count alone; this tier has no fixed iteration cap.
